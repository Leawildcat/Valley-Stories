## ⭐ Why Prairie Sage fits the Ranch
- **Native to the South Plains** (Texas Panhandle, eastern NM, western OK)  
- **Grows in dry, open prairie** — exactly where Jack’s ranch sits  
- **Used in Plains tribes’ medicine traditions** (Comanche, Kiowa, Apache)  
- **Different from the Valley’s mountain sage**  
- **Hardy, drought‑tolerant, and wind‑resistant**  
- **Smells sharper and more resinous than mountain sage**  
- **Used for smudging, cleansing, poultices, teas, and salves**  
- **Lea would gather it, not cultivate it**  
- **Jack would absolutely recognize it**  
- **It ties the Ranch into the tribal lineage without duplicating Valley flora**

## ⭐ What it looks like
- Silvery‑green leaves  
- Soft, fuzzy texture  
- Grows in clumps  
- About 1–3 feet tall  
- Thrives in sandy or caliche soils  
- Loves full sun and wind exposure
