1. The Spruce Grouse (aka “Fool Hen”) — BEST CHOICE for the Valley
This is the one I mentioned earlier, and it’s still the strongest option because:
Nickname: “fool hen”  
Why?
Because they are so tame and oblivious that early settlers said you could walk up and pick them up by hand.

It lives in the mountains
It behaves like a prairie chicken
It’s famously tame (to the point of stupidity)
It’s big enough to be a food source
It lays clutch eggs in shallow ground nests
It’s easy for Lea to gather from without harming the bird
Jack would absolutely torment them
Kaya would chase them
Mamma would give Jack the “don’t you dare” look

Eggs:  
Spruce grouse eggs are:

speckled
about the size of small chicken eggs
laid in clutches of 5–10
mild in flavor
perfect for baking or cooking
perfect for Lea to use in campfire meals

This is the most natural, believable, and story‑friendly option.
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spruce_grouse

2. The Dusky Grouse (Blue Grouse) — the “mountain chicken”
This bird is:

native to the Rockies
lives in high‑elevation forests
big, round, and chicken‑like
famously unbothered by humans
delicious (historically hunted for food)
slow enough that Jack could absolutely mess with one
calm enough that Kaya could herd them
common enough to be believable
pretty enough to feel magical

They have:

iridescent feathers
a deep, booming mating call
a habit of sitting in trees like confused chickens
a tendency to “freeze” instead of flee

These are larger than spruce grouse and very chicken‑like.

They:

live in high‑elevation forests
are slow and calm
lay large clutches of eggs
have mild‑tasting eggs

are historically used as food by mountain communities

Eggs:  
Bigger than spruce grouse eggs, still speckled, and very usable.

If you want a slightly more majestic bird, this is your pick.
https://www.audubon.org/field-guide/bird/dusky-grouse


3. The Ruffed Grouse — the drummer
These are smaller but still viable.

They:

hide in underbrush
explode upward when startled
taste like mild game bird
lay 8–12 eggs
nest on the ground
have beautifully speckled eggs
are tied to Indigenous folklore
have a distinctive “drumming” sound with their wings

If you want a bird with mythic resonance, this is the one.
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ruffed_grouse
https://www.allaboutbirds.org/guide/Ruffed_Grouse/lifehistory
