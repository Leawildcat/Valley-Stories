# Garden Crops

## Valley

### Winter

- Plant in Winter
  - Nothing

- Harvest in Winter
  - Carrots (overwintered, NOV–FEB)

### Spring

- Plant in Spring
  - Beets (MAR–APR)
  - Peas (MAR–APR)
  - Carrots - enough if going to overwinter (MAR–APR)
  - Potatoes (MAR–APR)
  - Kale (MAR–APR)
  - Spinach (MAR–APR)

- Harvest from Fall Over-Wintering
  - Carrots (overwintered, MAR–APR)

### Summer

- Plant in Summer
  - Nothing

- Harvest in Summer
  - Kale (JUN–AUG)
  - Spinach (JUN–JUL)
  - Carrots (JUN–AUG)
  - Beets (JUN–AUG)
  - Peas (JUN)
  - Potatoes (AUG)

### Fall

- Plant in Fall
  - Garlic (SEP–OCT)
  - Yellow Storage Onions (SEP–OCT)
  - Winter rye - _***Cover Crop***_ (SEP–OCT)
    - it protects the soil from erosion
    - it prevents nutrient loss
    - it suppresses weeds
    - it adds organic matter when cut
    - it improves spring planting beds
    - cut before seeding

- Harvest in Fall
  - Potatoes (SEP–OCT)
  - Beets (SEP–OCT)
  - Carrots (SEP–OCT)
  - Kale (SEP–NOV)
  - Spinach (SEP–NOV)
  - Peas (early SEP)

### Wild Gathered

- Berries (MAR–JUL)
  - Wild strawberries (MAR–MAY)
  - Juneberries / Serviceberries (MAY–JUN)
  - Currants — golden & wax (MAY–JUN)
  - Gooseberries (JUN–JUL)
  - Buffaloberries (JUN–JUL)
  - Thimbleberries (JUN–JUL)
  - Raspberries (JUN–JUL)
  - Blackberries — rare (JUN–JUL)
  - Chokecherries (JUL)
  - Huckleberries — normal & white (JUL)
  - Elderberries (JUL)

- Wild Fruits (APR–SEP)
  - Wild Plums (American Plum) (APR–JUN)
  - Wild Cherries (bird-scattered, naturalized) (MAY–JUL)
  - Crabapples (JUL–SEP)
  - Wild Apples (naturalized varieties) (AUG–SEP)
  - Hawthorn fruit (SEP)

- Roots & Bulbs (MAR–JUL)
  - Wild Onions (MAR–MAY)
  - Wild Garlic (MAR–MAY)
  - Spring Beauty roots (APR–JUN)
  - Cattail roots (MAY–JUL)

- Wild Greens & Flowers (MAR–SEP)
  - Dandelion greens (MAR–JUN)
  - Lamb’s Quarters (APR–SEP)
  - Dock / Sorrel (APR–JUL)
  - Spruce/Fir Tips (APR–JUN) ← citrusy syrup, jelly, tea
  - Wild Plum Blossoms (APR–MAY) ← jelly, syrup, floral water
  - Wild Apple Blossoms (APR–MAY) ← jelly, syrup, cider flavoring
  - Plantain leaves (MAY–SEP)
  - Mallow (MAY–SEP)
  - Nettle greens (MAY–JUN)
  - Wild Rose Petals (MAY–JUN) ← rose petal jelly, rose syrup
  - Currant Blossoms (MAY) ← floral syrup
  - Purslane (JUN–SEP)
  - Clover blossoms (JUN–SEP)
  - Rose hips (AUG–SEP)
  - Wild mint (JUL–SEP)
  - Honeysuckle blossoms (MAY–AUG)
    - Not native; introduced long ago by Native inhabitants or birds
    - The Valley accepted it and protects it
    - Now naturalized and part of the Valley’s scent signature
    - Nectar only enjoyed directly; not harvestable in bulk
    - Near nothern entrance from BLM campsite

- Wild Nuts & Seeds (JUN–SEP)
  - Amaranth seed (JUN–SEP)
  - Goosefoot seed — related to lamb’s quarters (JUL–SEP)
  - Sunflower seeds — wild varieties (AUG–SEP)
  - Gambel Oak acorns — processed for flour or must be leached first (SEP)
  - Pinyon Pine nuts (SEP)

- Wild Sap & Syrup (FEB–APR)
  - Rocky Mountain Maple sap (FEB–APR)
  - Boxelder sap (FEB–APR)
    - Sap is weaker than sugar maple; requires more boiling
    - Produces light, floral syrup
    - Gathered in small quantities for personal use
