1. Thimbleberries — the most “Lea” berry you could choose
These are REAL berries native to the Rockies, but almost nobody outside foragers knows about them.

They are:

soft
delicate
melt‑in‑your‑mouth
bright red
sweet‑tart
impossible to transport
only good when eaten or used immediately

Which means:

They are a perfect Valley berry.  
They don’t survive outside the Valley.
They don’t ship.
They don’t store.
They don’t freeze well.
They are ephemeral — like magic.

And the wine?

Thimbleberry wine is a thing.  
It’s floral, bright, and slightly wild.

This is the one I’d pick for Lea.
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rubus_parviflorus

⭐ 2. Serviceberries (Juneberries) — the “ancient” berry
These are also native to the Rockies and were used by Indigenous tribes for:

pemmican
syrups
wines
medicinal teas
They taste like:
blueberry
almond
and a hint of apple

They make a deep, rich wine that feels old, rooted, ancestral.

If you want a berry tied to the Valley’s history, this is the one.
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amelanchier

⭐ 3. Huckleberries — the “rare treasure”
Huckleberries grow in the Rockies but are famously:

hard to find
hard to cultivate
unpredictable
prized
and deeply flavorful

They make a dark, intense wine that feels almost enchanted.

If you want a berry that Jack steals and Lea guards, this is the one.
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Huckleberry

⭐ 4. Buffaloberries — the “mythic” berry
These are bright red, tart, and grow on silvery‑leafed shrubs.

They’re used in:

jellies
syrups
wines
ceremonial foods

They have a sharp, wild flavor that fits the Valley’s energy.

If you want something that feels a little dangerous, a little ancient, a little trickster‑aligned, this is the one.
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shepherdia
