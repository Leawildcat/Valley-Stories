# ⭐ The Sauce (the heart of the whole thing)
A real family sauce isn’t delicate.  
It’s **loud**, **bold**, and **made in a pot big enough to drown a toddler**.

It starts with:

- olive oil  
- a whole chopped onion  
- 6–8 cloves of garlic (minimum)  
- crushed red pepper if someone in the family likes heat  

Then:

- two big cans of crushed tomatoes  
- one can of tomato sauce  
- one can of tomato paste  
- a splash of red wine  
- a spoon of sugar (yes, they all do it)  
- basil, oregano, salt, pepper  
- bay leaf  

And then the secret:

**A parmesan rind**  
Every Italian family has one in the freezer.  
It melts into the sauce and makes it taste like someone’s grandmother blessed it.

Sauce simmers for **hours**.  
People walk by, dip bread in it, get yelled at, do it again.

---

# ⭐ The Meatballs (chaos in a bowl)
A real family meatball mix is messy:

- ground beef  
- ground pork  
- breadcrumbs  
- eggs  
- parmesan  
- parsley  
- garlic  
- salt  
- pepper  
- a splash of milk  

Everyone has an opinion on the ratio.  
Everyone is wrong except the person making them.

They get rolled by hand — uneven, imperfect, some too big, some too small — because that’s how you know they’re homemade.

They get browned in a pan, then dropped into the sauce to finish cooking.

The sauce turns into meatball perfume.

---

# ⭐ The Pasta (no one agrees on this either)
Spaghetti.  
Not angel hair.  
Not linguine.  
Not fettuccine.

**Spaghetti.**

Salt the water like the ocean.  
Cook it until it’s just shy of done.  
Finish it in a ladle of sauce so it clings.

Someone will argue about whether to mix the pasta and sauce or serve them separate.  
This is normal.
