# 🍁 **Which maples grow in northern New Mexico mountain valleys?**

There are **three** realistic candidates:

### **1. Rocky Mountain Maple (Acer glabrum)**  
- Native  
- Common in mixed conifer forests  
- Grows along streams, slopes, forest edges  
- Small tree, shrubby  
- **Can be tapped**  
- Sap is weaker than sugar maple but still sweet  
- Indigenous groups used it

### **2. Boxelder (Acer negundo)**  
- A maple species  
- Found in riparian areas  
- Very tap‑able  
- Sap is lower sugar but still usable  
- Often grows near meadows with seasonal water

### **3. Bigtooth Maple (Acer grandidentatum)**  
- Exists in pockets of NM  
- More common in Utah/Colorado  
- Can be tapped  
- Higher sugar content than Rocky Mountain maple

### **What does NOT grow in your Valley:**  
- Sugar maple  
- Red maple  
- Silver maple  
- Any eastern maple species

Those require lower elevation, richer soil, and more moisture.
